### Creating your workshop

So we’ve just given you the go ahead, what happens now? Now it’s time for the fun part, actually creating your workshop!

Let’s start by considering your audience. The majority of your audience will be beginners, and so you’ll need to keep this in mind when designing your slides.

You’ll need to guide your workshop participants through every step of the way, and we strongly advise that you explain the reasoning / logic behind some of the trickier steps!

The boba drops guide, designed by Lynn, is a great example of how you can explain the meaning behind different lines of code:

![alt](https://cdn.hackclub.com/019c4751-eb5e-7cb5-b906-d682f9469fa8/picture1.png) ![alt](https://cdn.hackclub.com/019c4751-e996-75d7-be0a-90cc97f84e25/picture2.png)

For CAD and PCB workshops, where the user is likely to use an unfamiliar program to make things, it can be helpful to point to different UI elements, either midway through a step, or at the start of the workshop, to save attendees struggling to find buttons.

Here’s an example, using Fusering, of how you can give a tour of the UI at the start of a workshop.

![alt](https://cdn.hackclub.com/019c4751-e7e0-7edc-acfa-f86f735f76bd/picture3.png) ![alt](https://cdn.hackclub.com/019c4751-e5df-7960-99e2-707b281a18f8/picture4.png)

When explaining steps, use simple language where possible! If you need to use a technical term, consider explaining it the first time that you use it!

So now that we know how to create steps and explain the meaning behind them, let’s now take a look at slide design!

Firstly decide on what software you’ll use to create your slides. Figma or Canva works great!

You may be tempted to use Powerpoint (or similar), however they tend to be less flexible that other options, and due to issues with fonts, you can’t guarantee that the powerpoint you create on your device, will look the same across all devices. They also tend to be more difficult to share!

A picture tells a 1000 words, so make sure your slides feature plenty of screenshots throughout! You might even want to consider GIFs for steps with multiple parts to them! The choice is yours!
Make sure your audience can read the information on the screen! Not too big, so that it is awkward to read on smaller devices, but not too small (we want the people at the back of the room to be able to read all of the text!).

Solid colours or patterned backgrounds work especially well for these slides, plus if you have a logo with certain colours or icons for your workshop, you can make your branding consistent across your slides. Don’t worry if you don’t have a logo yet, if invited to create a YSWS, we’ll help you to get one made!

Your workshop will likely end up on the big screen (typically smart boards, 4K in resolution). Make sure all of your assets, such as logos, graphics, and most importantly, screenshots, are all high resolution.

Avoid using the Hack Club logo at this stage. If we invite you to turn your workshop into a fully funded YSWS, then we’ll likely ask you to add it in, but at this stage, it could cause others to confuse your workshop for an official Hack Club program.
